Build good food

Michigan State University Extension defines 'good food' as food that is 'healthy, green, fair and affordable'.

We at fridgely disagree. 'Good food' doesn't have to be green, healthy, nor affordable (it's nice if it is though).
We see good food to be food that has to TASTE good!

fridgely helps turn ordinary food into 'good food'. Have you ever had a meal that consisted of a raw chicken, a sour lemon, and bowl of italian seasoning and pepper? I haven't, and surely would never want to. Actually, you probabl might die from eating such a meal as your stomach surely can't process raw chicken... Nonetheless, getting back to the point, fridgely helps provide you instructions to turn your bland and very individual ingredients into something spectacular!
